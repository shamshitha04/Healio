Possible emergency red flags include chest pain, pressure, or discomfort; trouble breathing; blue lips; sudden weakness or numbness; face drooping; sudden speech trouble; sudden confusion; sudden severe headache; severe bleeding; loss of consciousness; seizures; poisoning; severe allergic reaction; or thoughts of self-harm.

When emergency red flags are present, the safest response is to stop using an informational app and contact local emergency services immediately. AI-generated advice should not delay urgent care.

For possible stroke symptoms, time matters. Sudden face drooping, arm weakness, or speech difficulty should be treated as urgent. For possible heart attack symptoms such as chest pain with shortness of breath, sweating, nausea, or pain spreading to the arm, jaw, back, or shoulder, emergency services should be contacted.
